context: |
  PVL is an SMU overseas community service project running youth and community
  programmes in Vietnam. Going into the cycle, fundraising decks, leader
  recruitment posts, and on-ground collateral were each pulling from a
  different look — no consistent mark, no system. I built the visual identity
  from scratch: a primary mark plus themed lockups around the on-ground
  programme strands (mountain treks, banh mi, egg coffee).
role: |
  Owned the identity end-to-end. Designed the logo system in Illustrator,
  art-directed the leaders photoshoot, and specced the shirt prints for the
  cohort.
outcome: |
  Shipped as the brand standard for the cycle — used across fundraising,
  leader recruitment, and the marketing rollout. Replaced three prior ad-hoc
  looks with one durable mark the team could apply themselves.
